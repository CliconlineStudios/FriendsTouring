Gay Friendly

We offer many LGBT travel and tourism options for all tastes, preferences and budget. Gay-friendly cruise to Galapagos. What is the best time to travel? Thanks to its position on the equator, there is never a bad time to enjoy a gay-friendly cruise to the Galapagos. There is always plenty to see and do, but if you have certain preferences about what you want to see or which activities you particularly enjoy, there may be a "sweet spot" time for your personal needs. The warm season runs from December to May, with average temperatures of 30°C and a generally warmer and calmer sea. It is the best time for diving enthusiasts, since the sea temperatures mean that it is not always necessary to wear a neoprene suit (or maybe just a swimsuit). The cold season runs from June to November, and high temperatures are likely to be around 25°C, with cloudier skies and the occasional shower. The sea conditions are a little harsher, with higher waves, and the sea temperatures are lower thanks to the cold currents. On the other hand, these cold currents are rich in food, so you can see more marine species at this time. You will need a wetsuit.

CLIMBING RUCU PICHINCHA IN QUITO

Climbing Rucu Pichincha in Quito is a great opportunity for hikers to acclimatize without venturing far from the city. It is literally a stone's throw from the center of town. At 4,784 m, it is quite high by South African and even European standards. But here, it's just a bump in an extraordinary landscape.

Getting to the start of the Climb

Getting there is quite easy up the Teleférico (cable car from Quito). Here we buy our tickets and take a 15 minute ride to the top. The journey is impressive and the views of this city are incredible.

Route to the summit

Once at the top station (Cruz Loma, 4050m), you will find two main routes leading to the summit. The first is an exciting scramble along the ridge through El Paso De la Muerte. This is a fairly straightforward fight, but it has an exposed journey. The second route forks to the right and goes around the "back" of the volcano. This route is less dangerous, but it is necessary to climb the sandy ash slopes for about 45 minutes. On a clear day, you can see three 5000m volcanoes, including the Cotopaxi Volcano.

This is an amazing climbing adventure in Quito.!!

What to take for Rucu Pichincha

Two liters of water, some light snacks, waterproof jacket, rigid walking shoes, gloves and walking poles. Bring sunscreen and sunglasses, even if it's cloudy. Important: 2 days of acclimatization are needed in Quito before attempting this peak.

EXPLORE the Cueva de los Tayos

A mythical place like few others, most likely visiting it will move you to the bottom of your being. The Cave is located within the independent territory of the Shuar, so you will need to coordinate your entry with the community. You can get there on foot, on horseback, by boat and there is even the option of doing it by helicopter. But getting there is not the only exciting thing… what really requires courage is going down the Chimney or vertical tunnel, which implies a 90 degree descent of almost 50 meters downwards. It is done with climbing rope, harness and descenders. You dare? Some visitors also take the opportunity to try Ayahuasca.!

The man who stepped on the Moon descends to the depths of the Earth And this is where the famous astronaut Neil Armstrong enters the scene. Together with a large group of experts, the American (who in 1969 had gone down in history as the first man to set foot on the Moon) arrived in Ecuador to visit the enigmatic cave.

Armstrong arrived in South America at the invitation of the expedition leader, Scottish engineer Stanley Hall. Such a famous guest took advantage of the trip to descend into the caves in August 1976.
